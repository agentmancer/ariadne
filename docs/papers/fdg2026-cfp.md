# FDG 2026 Call for Papers

**21st ACM Foundations of Digital Games Conference**
**Copenhagen, Denmark | August 10-14, 2026**

## Theme: "Research through Games; Research for Games"

This theme highlights a core strength of FDG: bridging the gap between scientific and humanistic approaches to games research by exploring the dual role of games as both objects of study and powerful tools for discovery.

## Submission Tracks

1. **Technical Game Development** - Engines, graphics, networking, VR/AR
2. **Game Design, Studio Practices, and Novel Player Experiences** - Design methodology and aesthetics
3. **Game Analytics and Visualization** - Data science and player behavior analysis
4. **Game Artificial Intelligence** - AI for gameplay, design, and procedural content
5. **Game Criticism and Analysis** - Humanities-informed perspectives
6. **Games Beyond Entertainment** - Serious games and educational applications
7. **Games Pedagogy** - Teaching games and game development
8. **Generative AI** - AI applications in game development and analysis

## Paper Formats & Page Limits

- **Full Papers:** Up to 10 pages (two columns, excluding references/appendices)
- **Short/Late-Breaking Papers:** Up to 6 pages (one column) or 4 pages (two columns)
- **Abstracts:** Up to 500 words; accepted abstracts receive oral slots but aren't published

## Review Criteria

Papers are evaluated on:
- **Originality and novelty** of contribution
- **Technical/methodological soundness**
- **Clarity and quality of presentation**
- **Relevance to FDG community and selected track**
- **Reproducibility** (where applicable)
- **Significance of contribution** to the field

## Key Requirements

- **Anonymization:** Remove all identifying author/institution information (double-blind review)
- **Template:** ACM SIGCONF Master Template required
- **Submission Portal:** EasyChair (https://easychair.org/conferences?conf=fdg26)
- **Review Process:** Double-anonymized; three reviews per paper plus meta-review

- Abstract Deadline: December 5, 2025
- **Paper Submission: December 15, 2025**
- Notifications: February 1, 2026
- Late-Breaking/Doctoral Consortium: March 30, 2026
- Camera-Ready: TBD

## Topics of Interest

FDG welcomes research on all aspects of digital games including but not limited to:

- Game AI and procedural content generation
- Player modeling and analytics
- Game design patterns and methodologies
- Interactive narrative and storytelling
- Serious games and games for learning
- Game user research and player experience
- Game engines and technical infrastructure
- VR/AR/XR gaming
- Multiplayer and social gaming
- Game accessibility
- Ethics in games and game research
- Cultural and critical perspectives on games

## Other Submission Categories

- **Games and Demos**: Playable submissions showcasing research
- **Doctoral Consortium**: For PhD students to present and receive feedback
- **Workshops**: Half or full-day focused sessions
- **Competitions**: Game AI competitions and challenges
- **Panels**: Discussion sessions on timely topics
